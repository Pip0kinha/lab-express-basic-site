W O R K

“…failure with clay was more complete and more spectacular than with other forms of art. You are subject to the elements…Any one of the old four – earth, air, fire, water – can betray you and melt, or burst, or shatter – months of work into dust and ashes and spitting steam. You need to be a precise scientist, and you need to know how to play with what chance will do to your lovingly constructed surfaces in the heat of the kiln”.

- A.S.Byatt

My work consists mostly of functional tablewear, but sometimes I also create decorative vessels. At this point I do not produce pottery for sale.

I've been gifting my pieces to my friends and family and they have now taken my work to Portugal, Georgia, Germany, Austria, Brazil, Italy and Indonesia. It amuses me to know that pieces that I create are spread around the word, and that they are used on a daily basis by people that I love and care about.

To me, sharing my work feels like sharing a piece of me that contains fractions of my learnings, mistakes, experiments, time and soul.
