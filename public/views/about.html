A B O U T // L E T I C I A

"I am committed to the intangible and intuitive aspects of art. Through the things I make I give people some insight into how I see. I believe that all objects contain energy that embodies a feeling."

- Joanna Powell

I've always been interested in art and creating with my hands, however during my adult life I silenced those passions and disconnected myself from art. It was only during the pandemic that I rediscovered art and found my way to pottery.

My first contact with pottery was in Bali, Indonesia where I learned about the handbuilding techniques of pinching, coiling and slab rolling. With those techniques I started creating vessels and sculptures.

Soon after, I discovered the potter's wheel and went to Tuscany, Italy to aquire the abilities required to produce functional tablewear with the potter's wheel.

During my time in Italy I also learned more about the steps of pottery that follow the building of the pieces: Glazing and Firing. I've learned how to create my own glazing recipes and further experiment with this step of the process.

I've also learn about the differences between reduction and oxidation fire and had the priviledge to experience two wood fires - which is the oldest method of firing clay. The work fired in this type of kiln takes a full day of stoking (putting wood in the kiln) in 5-10 minute intervals in order to achieve a maximum temperature of 1200 degrees, making the clay vitrify or harden into a glass.
